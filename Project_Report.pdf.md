This project focuses on developing digital literacy skills, including understanding online tools, building a professional presence, practicing coding, learning communication etiquette, and staying safe from cybercrime. Each task helped in improving practical knowledge and awareness of digital platforms.

## Task 1

For Task 1, I created an infographic on the topic “Digital Literacy for Students.” The infographic explains the meaning of digital literacy, which includes using digital tools effectively, communicating online properly, and finding reliable information.

It also highlights safe internet practices such as not sharing personal information, using strong passwords, avoiding unknown links, and being careful on social media platforms. In addition, I included some useful digital tools for students like Google Docs, GitHub, Canva, and Google Drive.

This task helped me understand the importance of digital literacy and how it plays a key role in safe and effective use of technology in daily life.


## Task 2

For Task 2, I created and updated my digital profiles on GitHub and LinkedIn to build a professional online presence. GitHub is a platform used by developers to store and manage code, while LinkedIn is a professional networking platform used to showcase education, skills, and achievements.

On GitHub, I created a profile README that includes my name, branch, and a short introduction about my interest in programming and technology. This will help me showcase my projects and track my progress in coding over time. On LinkedIn, I added my educational details, including my degree and institution, to make my profile more professional and visible to others.

In the next four years, I plan to regularly update these platforms by adding my projects, achievements, and skills. These platforms will help me build a strong digital portfolio, connect with professionals, and improve my career opportunities.


## Task 3

For Task 3, I explored coding and collaboration platforms. I used HackerRank to practice coding by solving a beginner-level problem called “Solve Me First,” which helped me understand basic input-output and logic building in programming.

For collaboration, I created a Google Form titled “Digital Literacy Quiz” with five questions, including multiple choice and short answer types. I also collected responses by submitting the form myself and viewed the results using Google Sheets.

These platforms will help me improve my coding skills and collaborate effectively in academic projects. HackerRank will strengthen my problem-solving abilities, while Google Forms and Sheets will help me collect and analyze data efficiently during my studies.


## Task 4

For Task 4, I learned about email etiquette and professional communication. I drafted two formal emails: one for requesting an extension for an assignment and another for applying for a summer internship.

While writing these emails, I followed proper email structure, including subject line, greeting, clear body, and a polite closing. I also ensured that the tone of the emails was formal and respectful.

Additionally, I created a list of social media do’s and don’ts to understand responsible online behavior. This helped me learn how to use digital platforms safely and professionally.

This task improved my communication skills and helped me understand the importance of professionalism in digital communication.


## Task 5

For Task 5, I studied a cybercrime case related to UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud, which is very common in digital transactions today. In this type of fraud, attackers trick users into sharing their UPI PIN or clicking on malicious links by pretending to be from banks or payment services.

The victim is often misled into believing that entering a UPI PIN is required to receive money, while in reality, it is only needed to send money. Once the PIN is entered, money is transferred from the victim’s account to the attacker’s account without their knowledge.

To prevent such cybercrimes, it is important to follow safe digital practices such as never sharing UPI PINs, avoiding unknown links, verifying payment requests, and using only trusted applications. Awareness and careful usage of digital platforms can help in reducing such incidents and ensuring online safety.


Overall, this project helped me understand the importance of digital literacy in modern life. I learned how to use digital tools effectively, communicate professionally, and stay safe online. These skills will be useful for my academic and professional growth in the future.
